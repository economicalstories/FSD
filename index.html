Financial Stability — Early Warning Dashboard

A prototype framework for proactively monitoring the financial sustainability of Australian Government non-corporate and corporate Commonwealth entities — surfacing leading indicators of financial pressure for earlier, targeted engagement rather than reactive crisis management.

Ratio-based indicators

Each indicator is ranked against peers in the comparison group, not against a fixed threshold.

Each indicator states its formula and the exact financial-statement line items it draws on, then shows where this entity sits in the distribution of its peers — the rank chip (top-right) reads n of N from the less-favourable end, and the strip plots this entity (dot) against the peer median (line) and middle-50% (box). No single indicator is determinative, and a rank is a prompt to investigate, never a conclusion. Pre-loaded figures are real, sourced and editable.

Data quality & provenance

Read before interpreting anything above.

Where the numbers come from

  • Entity register — Dept. of Finance PGPA Flipchart (1 March 2024): 101 non-corporate + 74 corporate Commonwealth entities. The 16 Commonwealth companies are out of scope.
  • Financial figures — audited financial statements in each entity's annual report on the Transparency Portal.
  • Published ratios — the Portal's own financial-sustainability ratios & data explorer.
  • Audit findings — ANAO financial-statement audit reports.
  • GFS sector (GGS / PNFC / PFC) — PGPA Flipchart markers, defined by the Dept. of Finance glossary (GGS, PNFC, PFC); the authoritative list of non-GGS entities is Budget Paper No. 1, Statement 10, Appendix A.
  • Entity status (active / defunct) — Administrative Arrangements Orders & the Machinery of Government Changes Guide.

Known limitations

  • REAL DATAFigures for ~169 entities are pre-loaded from audited financial statements on the Transparency Portal — zero synthetic data; every figure clicks through to its source and is editable.
  • TIMELINESSAudited data is annual and lags ~3–5 months after 30 June. In-year data is sparse and inconsistent.
  • COMPARABILITYMergers, machinery-of-government changes and reclassifications break historical comparability.

Interpreting relative standing

  • Indicators are read relative to peers in the chosen comparison group — a position in the distribution, not a pass/fail label. The illustrative absolute thresholds are shown for context only.
  • Ranking (1 = furthest toward the less-favourable end) prompts investigation; it is never a conclusion about an entity.
  • Compare like with like — corporate entities, RDCs and financial corporations behave very differently from departments, which is why the default group is the entity's own category.
