Financial Stability — Early Warning Dashboard

A prototype framework for proactively monitoring the financial sustainability of Australian Government non-corporate and corporate Commonwealth entities — surfacing leading indicators of financial pressure for earlier, targeted engagement rather than reactive crisis management.

Data quality & provenance

Read before interpreting anything above.

Where the numbers come from

  • Entity register — Dept. of Finance PGPA Flipchart (1 March 2024): 101 non-corporate + 74 corporate Commonwealth entities. The 16 Commonwealth companies are out of scope.
  • Financial figures — audited financial statements in each entity's annual report on the Transparency Portal.
  • Published ratios — the Portal's own financial-sustainability ratios & data explorer.
  • Audit findings — ANAO financial-statement audit reports.

Known limitations

  • NO DATANothing financial is pre-loaded. This tool ships with zero synthetic data — every figure must be entered from, and verified against, its public source.
  • TIMELINESSAudited data is annual and lags ~3–5 months after 30 June. In-year data is sparse and inconsistent.
  • COMPARABILITYMergers, machinery-of-government changes and reclassifications break historical comparability.

Interpreting flags

  • Thresholds are illustrative defaults for demonstration, not endorsed benchmarks.
  • A flag is a prompt to investigate and read context — never a conclusion.
  • Corporate entities (trading bodies, RDCs, financial corporations) behave very differently from departments; compare like with like.

Prototype demonstration only — not for reliance. This dashboard is an early-warning framework built for demonstration. It draws solely on public information and contains no dummy or synthetic financial data; every quantitative element is either entered by the user from a cited public source or left blank pending that source. It does not constitute financial advice or any assessment of any entity's financial position. The Australian Government, the Department of Finance and the ANAO do not endorse this tool.
